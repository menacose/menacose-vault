## Funding

- Base Funding: The chapter receives a base funding amount of $1,000.
    
- Membership Dues: INCOSE pays the chapter 21% of regular membership dues and 15% for seniors and students.
    
- Local Fundraising: Chapters can raise funds locally; these money are 100% allocated to the chapter.
    

## Fund Balance & Allocation

- Reporting: Chapter shares are allocated twice yearly, in July and January.
    
- Tracking: A report is emailed during allocation months, or earlier if there are specific activities.
    

## Accounting Support

- Centralized Support: INCOSE supports chapter accounting, including accounts payable and receivable.
    
- Finance Contact: Priscila Norat (Finance and Administration Manager) assists with banking and account balance items.
    

## Expense Process

- Internal Approval: Chapters must follow internal processes for budgeting and approving expenses.
    
- Payment Processing: Once approved, documents should be emailed to accounting@incose.net for processing (1–5 business days).
    
- Documentation: Vendors require an invoice; reimbursements require a report and receipts.

## MENACOSE Chapter Finance Q&A

Question: Is there a way to see the balance of the funds available to the chapter?

Answer: At this point you have $1,000 of initial funds and we will allocate chapter shares for you twice a year, in July and January. We’ll email you a report in those months, or earlier if you have other activities before that.

  

Question: How much of the membership fees are allocated to the chapter?

Answer: In general, 21% for regular membership and 15% for seniors and students.

  

Question: What was the starter amount for the chapter?

Answer: $1,000.

  

Question: What is the process of using the available funds?

Answer: Chapters should have an internal process for budgeting and approving expenses. Once internally approved, email documents to accounting@incose.net. Required documents include invoices for vendor payments or a reimbursement report with receipts for reimbursements.

Question: Can the chapter raise funds through other activities and deposit them in the account?

Answer: Yes. Local money raised are 100% allocated to the chapter.

  

Question: What level of discretion does a virtual chapter have over financial decisions?

Answer: All local decisions are made locally. The chapter decides on events and budgets. INCOSE acts as the bank but provides added support like legal reviews on contracts.
